A little more about me:




I have been passionate about computers for many years, and am excited to have been given the opportunity with SUSD to explore this further. Not only have I been able to learn more about the hardware side of technology and troubleshooting with a variety of classroom systems, I have built important relationships. Being able to solve the problems of teachers and other school staff brings me joy as I know the work I do makes a direct impact. Helping support teachers teach and seeing the help I can bring further encourages me to learn more and more about technology, so I can continue to do this.

Prior to SUSD, I had no professional tech experience and instead largely studied subjects and participated in robotics. This was where my love for technology was sparked. I first started in FLL in 2016 while in elementary school as part of the Goodman Gators.Throughout middle school I took STEM classes and participated in Vex, but what made me most excited was my high school participation in FIRST.

I spent four years programming on the robotics team, and it inspired me to fully commit to computer science. Even though I did learn valuable hard skills revolving around Java and GitHub, I learned a great deal more of life skills, such as leadership and efficient teamwork. I learned how to troubleshoot, working through tricky problems, and how to research to avoid getting stuck. It encouraged a love for problem solving that I take with me in my day to day life.

Because of this experience, it is driving me to continually learn more about computers. I am in the process of becoming a mentor for my old robotics team so I can continue to learn and share my love for the community, but I am also building knowledge outside of this. I am working to get the CompTIA trio, and will follow this with the CCNA certifications. I am improving my programming every day. And above all else, continuing to find new problems to solve and new things to learn.
